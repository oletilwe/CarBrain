    SLR

    The Mercedes-Benz SLR McLaren is a grand tourer jointly developed by German automotive manufacturer Mercedes-Benz and British automobile manufacturer McLaren Automotive and sold from 2003 to 2010. When the car was developed, Mercedes-Benz owned 40 per cent of the McLaren Group and the car was produced in conjunction between the two companies. The car was offered in coupé, roadster and speedster bodystyles with the latter being a limited edition model.

    The SLR has a hand-built 5.4L supercharged V8 that has a power output of 617 hp 780 N⋅m. The SLR uses the AMG SPEEDSHIFT R five-speed automatic transmission with three manual modes. The SLR features active aerodynamics; there is a spoiler mounted on the rear integral air brake flap.
