    Things you didn't know pt.1

    This section will be separated in to different section accross the whole blog it will be filled with information that i just thought people should know and might need one day.

    There is a difference between All wheel drive (AWD) and 4 wheel drive (4WD), yes they both technically do the same thing which is to improve a vehicle's traction and handling in various driving conditions.

    AWD:

    • AWD is always on meaning power is continuesly supplied to the wheels and it is often designed to optimize on-road traction and handling in various weather conditions.
    • AWD systems are suitable for improving traction in wet, snowy, or slippery conditions. AWD is not always optimized for challenging off-road terrain.
    • AWD systems are more fuel effecient and are generally simpler and lighter than 4WD systems
    • AWD systems typically distribute power automatically between the front and rear wheels based on traction needs. Some advanced AWD systems can also distribute power between individual wheels for enhanced control

    4WD

    • 4WD systems are often manually engaged or can be automatically activated by the driver and 4WD is commonly found in off-road vehicles, trucks, and SUVs.
    • 4WD can provide additional traction when driving on rough terrain or through mud and snow.
    • 4WD systems tend to be more complex, with additional components like transfer cases and locking differentials
    • In 4WD systems, power is often evenly distributed to both the front and rear wheels. Some 4WD systems allow the driver to manually adjust the distribution of power between the front and rear axles.

    These are some of the differences between the 2 systems.
